Official Rulings (Updated as of 6/2/2023)

Effects that are triggered as a result of actions (such as printing, mixing, or refilling) occur immediately after that action has resolved. This has several timing implications:
  1. Jungle: If a player Refills and returns Jungle to the hand, Jungle will not activate its effect to “add a Rank I card to the hand” because it will no longer be in the Play Area by the time Refill resolves.
  2. Orange: When an Orange in play is used to mix for something else, it will have left the play area by the time the Mix resolved. Thus, you cannot print the resulting card.
  3. Coral + Lemon: When a Coral in the play area is used for a mix, a second mix (via Lemon) can no longer use cards in the play area for the mix since Coral is no longer in play.
  4. Violet + any card that adds cards to hand: Violet allows a player to print 2 additional times in a turn. Each print and resulting effects are resolved one by one. Thus, if the first additional print is used to print a card such as Cerulean, then the cards Cerulean adds to the hand can be used for the second print action.
  5. Orange + Lemon: The same logic applies: Mixing and printing a card like Turquoise will allow the second mix to use the cards added from Turquoise.
  6. Orange into Lemon: When an Orange is in play and Lemon is mixed and printed, that player can then mix a second time as Lemon allows 2 mixes per turn.
Other common rulings:
  1. Pear: Pear triggers itself.
  2. Coral: A Coral in play can be used in a mix.
  3. Solo Mode Tiebreaker Rules: On the lowest difficulty, Confident Billy goes second, so they will get a turn after you meet your win condition to potentially win on ties. On higher difficulty modes, Confident Billy goes first, so you will get an extra turn after Billy meets their win condition to beat them on tiebreakers.
